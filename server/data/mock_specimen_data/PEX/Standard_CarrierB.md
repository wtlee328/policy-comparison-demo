# Specimen Policy: General Partner and Portfolio Liability (GPPL)
**Form Number:** CB-GPPL-2024-SPEC-V1
**Carrier:** Carrier B
**Form Type:** Industry Standard

## SECTION I. THE INSURING AGREEMENTS

### 1.1 General Partner Liability
The Underwriter shall pay on behalf of the General Partner all Loss resulting from a Claim for a Wrongful Act by the General Partner or any Insured Person.

### 1.2 Portfolio Company Board Liability
The Underwriter shall pay on behalf of an Insured Person all Loss for which such Insured Person is not indemnified by the Portfolio Company, arising from a Claim for a Wrongful Act committed in their capacity as a director of such Portfolio Company.

### 1.3 Entity Professional Liability
The Underwriter shall pay on behalf of the Firm all Loss resulting from a Claim first made against the Firm for a Wrongful Act by the Firm in the performance of Private Equity Services.

## SECTION II. DEFINITIONS

**“Private Equity Services”** means the management of one or more Funds, the solicitation of Limited Partners, the selection of Portfolio Companies, and the provision of advice to Portfolio Companies.

**“Insured Person”** means any natural person who was, is, or shall be:
(a) a Manager, Officer, or Partner of the General Partner;
(b) a member of the Firm’s Investment Committee;
(c) any "Board Observer" at a Portfolio Company, but only for acts committed in that capacity.

**“Portfolio Company”** means any corporation, partnership, or other entity in which a Fund (managed by the General Partner) owns an interest.

**“Wrongful Act”** means any actual or alleged act, error, omission, neglect, or breach of duty.

## SECTION III. EXCLUSIONS

### 3.1 Dishonest or Fraudulent Acts
The Underwriter is not liable for Loss arising from any Insured's dishonest or fraudulent act. This exclusion requires a "Final Adjudication" against the Insured. Severability exists such that the act of one Insured Person is not imputed to any other Insured Person.

### 3.2 Improper Profit or Advantage
Excludes any Loss arising from the receipt of any profit to which the Insured was not legally entitled.

### 3.3 Investment Banking and Portfolio Operations
No coverage for (i) the issuance or underwriting of securities for third parties, or (ii) the day-to-day manufacturing, distribution, or retail operations of any Portfolio Company.

## SECTION IV. ENDORSEMENTS

**Endorsement CB-GPPL-10: Spousal and Estate Coverage**
Extends the definition of Insured Person to include the estate, heirs, and legal representatives of a deceased or insolvent Insured Person.

**Endorsement CB-GPPL-15: Notice of Acquisition**
New Portfolio Companies are automatically covered if the Fund's investment is <$50M or <25% of the Portfolio Company's total equity.
