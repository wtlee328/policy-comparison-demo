# Specimen Policy: Investment Adviser Professional Liability (IAPL)
**Form Number:** MCA-IAPL-100 (01/24)
**Carrier:** Carrier A
**Form Type:** Industry Standard

## I. INSURING AGREEMENTS

### (A) Individual Professional Liability
The Insurer shall pay, on behalf of each Insured Person, Loss resulting from any Claim first made against such Insured Person during the Policy Period (or the Discovery Period, if applicable) for a Wrongful Act in the performance of or failure to perform Professional Services by such Insured Person, but only when and to the extent that the Company has not indemnified such Insured Person for such Loss.

### (B) Company Professional Liability
The Insurer shall pay, on behalf of the Company, Loss resulting from any Claim first made against the Company during the Policy Period (or the Discovery Period, if applicable) for a Wrongful Act in the performance of or failure to perform Professional Services by the Company or by any Insured Person.

### (C) Company Indemnification Excellence
The Insurer shall pay, on behalf of the Company, Loss resulting from any Claim first made against an Insured Person during the Policy Period (or the Discovery Period, if applicable) for a Wrongful Act in the performance of or failure to perform Professional Services, but only when and to the extent that the Company has indemnified such Insured Person for such Loss.

### (D) Defense and Settlement
The Insurer shall have the right and duty to defend any Claim seeking Loss which is covered by this Policy, even if any or all of the allegations in such Claim are groundless, false, or fraudulent. The Insurer’s duty to defend shall cease upon exhaustion of the applicable Limit of Liability. The Insurer may, with the written consent of the Insured, settle any Claim as the Insurer deems expedient. If the Insured refuses to consent to a settlement recommended by the Insurer, the Insurer’s liability for Loss shall not exceed the amount for which the Claim could have been settled plus Defense Costs incurred up to the date of such refusal.

## II. DEFINITIONS

**“Claim”** means:
(1) a written demand for monetary, non-monetary, or injunctive relief;
(2) a civil, criminal, administrative, regulatory, or formal investigative proceeding; 
(3) a formal investigative order, or a Wells Notice issued by the SEC or equivalent regulatory body,
provided that a Claim shall not include any routine audit, examination, or compliance-related inquiry.

**“Insured Person”** means any natural person who was, is, or shall become:
(1) a partner, principal, officer, director, or trustee of the Company;
(2) a member of the Board of Managers or Advisory Board of the Company;
(3) an Employee of the Company while acting in a professional capacity; or
(4) an independent contractor, but only while acting under the direction and supervision of the Company and only for Professional Services.

**“Loss”** means damages, settlements, judgments, and Defense Costs. Loss shall not include:
(1) fines, penalties, or taxes imposed by law;
(2) amounts for which the Insured is not legally liable;
(3) matters deemed uninsurable under the law of the jurisdiction in which the Claim is brought; or
(4) the return, restitution, or disgorgement of fees, profits, or charges.

**“Professional Services”** means services performed by the Company for a Client of the Company for a fee in the conduct of its business as a registered investment adviser under the Investment Advisers Act of 1940. Professional Services includes the management of portfolios, the provision of investment advice, and the selection of third-party sub-advisers, but excludes any services as a broker-dealer or custodian.

**“Wrongful Act”** means any actual or alleged breach of duty, neglect, error, misstatement, misleading statement, or omission.

## III. EXCLUSIONS

The Insurer shall not be liable for Loss in connection with any Claim:

### (A) Conduct
Based upon, arising out of, or in any way involving any criminal, fraudulent, or malicious act, or any willful violation of statute. This exclusion shall only apply if there is a final, non-appealable adjudication in the underlying proceeding or a separate proceeding establishing such conduct. This exclusion shall not apply to Defense Costs, which shall be advanced until such final adjudication.

### (B) Personal Profit
Based upon, arising out of, or in any way involving any Insured gaining any profit, remuneration, or financial advantage to which such Insured was not legally entitled. This exclusion shall apply only if there is a final, non-appealable adjudication establishing such advantage.

### (C) Bodily Injury and Property Damage
For bodily injury, sickness, disease, or death of any person, or damage to or destruction of any tangible property.

### (D) ERISA
For any actual or alleged violation of the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974 (ERISA).

## IV. ENDORSEMENTS

**Endorsement CA-01: Cyber Liability Exclusion (Amended)**
This Policy does not apply to any Claim based upon or arising out of a Cyber Attack or unauthorized access to the Company’s computer systems.

**Endorsement CA-02: Sanctions Limitation Clause**
No coverage shall be provided for any Insured who is a Specially Designated National (SDN).
